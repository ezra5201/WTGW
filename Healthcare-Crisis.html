Healthcare Crisis

Medical facilities, hospitals, and healthcare infrastructure under siege

The systematic targeting and destruction of Gaza's healthcare infrastructure represents one of the most devastating aspects of the conflict. This documentation tracks the impact on medical facilities, healthcare workers, and civilian access to essential medical care across Gaza.

Timeline of Healthcare Infrastructure Attacks

October 12, 2024
Al-Shifa Hospital, Gaza's largest medical complex, comes under direct attack. Multiple buildings damaged, emergency services disrupted.
October 18, 2024
Indonesian Hospital in northern Gaza forced to evacuate patients. Intensive care unit shut down due to power cuts and safety concerns.
November 3, 2024
Al-Quds Hospital surrounded by forces. Medical staff and patients trapped inside for 48 hours without power or water.
November 15, 2024
Kamal Adwan Hospital in northern Gaza reports direct hits to pediatric and maternity wards. 12 newborns evacuated under emergency conditions.
December 1, 2024
WHO reports only 16 of Gaza's 36 hospitals remain partially functional. Critical shortage of medical supplies and fuel for generators.

Impact on Civilian Healthcare

  • Chronic disease patients unable to access regular treatment and medication
  • Emergency surgeries cancelled or performed without adequate facilities
  • Pregnant women forced to give birth without medical assistance
  • Children unable to receive vaccinations and routine medical check-ups
  • Mental health services completely overwhelmed and under-resourced
"We are working in conditions that no medical professional should ever face. We have performed surgeries by flashlight, delivered babies in bomb shelters, and watched patients die from treatable conditions because we lack basic supplies. The systematic destruction of our healthcare system is not just an attack on buildings—it's an attack on the right to life itself."
— Dr. Sarah Ahmed, Emergency Physician at Al-Shifa Hospital

International Response

Multiple international organizations have documented and condemned the attacks on healthcare facilities:


World Health Organization (WHO): Declared the healthcare system in Gaza has "collapsed" and called for immediate protection of medical facilities under international humanitarian law.


Médecins Sans Frontières (Doctors Without Borders): Reported that their medical teams have been forced to evacuate multiple facilities and condemned the targeting of healthcare infrastructure as a violation of international law.


International Committee of the Red Cross: Called for safe corridors to allow medical supplies and personnel to reach hospitals, emphasizing that healthcare facilities must be protected during conflict.
